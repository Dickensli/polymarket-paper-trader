## Executive summary

Server evidence confirms that strategy-specific paper execution gates are active and eligible paper trades can still fill. The recurring failure was instead in the language-model control flow: Kalshi paper reports repeatedly converted unmet graduation metrics into a persistent `STRICT DEFENSIVE HALT`, then later cycles read those conclusions as if they were current risk state. The previous emergency fix suppressed all reports during trading bootstrap, which prevented recurrence but also removed the intended cross-session learning feature.

This revision restores bounded report reading for all six mapped prompts while retaining a hard trust boundary. Updated MCP registration starts `report-memory-v2` exactly once; the server records the activation timestamp, legacy reports remain available to human audit, and agent context/list/read operations expose only reports created after that timestamp. Kalshi paper strategies query graduation every cycle only after current trade/no-trade decisions and execution verification. Graduation remains notification-only for paper trading.

No thresholds, schedules, modes, IDs, account bindings, or risk limits changed. No reports or production rows were deleted.
